Audine’s Story

A Sinful, Malevolent, Deceptive Narrative

Psychological Fiction 1930s America Historical

Follows Audine Collins on her 1933 journey from New Orleans to Chicago. A complex character study of ambition, bitterness, and the consequences of personal choices. Through her eyes, the reader witnesses the harsh realities of the Great Migration and the unforgiving nature of those who refuse to be held back.

Setting: New Orleans → Chicago, 1933
Themes: Ambition, Betrayal, Identity, Consequence

About the Author

Sondra J. Hardy

Born and raised in West Haven, Connecticut, Sondra J. Hardy is a writer whose work traces the hidden tensions within families and communities. A graduate of West Haven High School and Bay State College, where she earned the Beatrice Ruderman Award, Hardy brings a historian's eye and a storyteller's patience to her narratives.

Her literary work, influenced by authors like Walter Mosley, explores themes of secrecy, betrayal, and faith across interconnected urban landscapes — from the humid streets of New Orleans to the bustling corridors of Chicago and the cultural pulse of Harlem.

Hardy's fiction inhabits the shadows of American history, giving voice to those who live in the margins and whose stories refuse to be forgotten.
